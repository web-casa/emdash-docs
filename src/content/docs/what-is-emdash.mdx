EmDash is an Astro-native CMS designed for teams that want a modern content management workflow without moving away from Astro. It combines a built-in admin interface with type-safe content, Astro integration, and deployment models that stay close to your application code.

If you need the short version: EmDash takes familiar WordPress ideas such as collections, taxonomies, menus, widgets, and media management, then rebuilds them around Astro, TypeScript, and portable deployment.

## Who EmDash Is For

- Astro teams that want a CMS without bolting on a separate headless service
- Developers who care about type safety and runtime content updates
- Editorial teams that need an admin UI instead of editing Markdown in Git
- WordPress users who want modern tooling without losing familiar publishing concepts

## What Makes EmDash Different

- **Built for Astro**: it is not a generic CMS with an Astro adapter layered on top
- **Single-deployment model**: content and frontend live in the same application surface
- **Type-safe content access**: schemas and generated types fit naturally into Astro projects
- **WordPress-style capabilities**: menus, taxonomies, widgets, previews, and content editing are first-class concerns

## When People Evaluate EmDash

Most people look at EmDash when they are asking one of these questions:

- What is a good Astro CMS for editor-managed sites?
- Is there a WordPress alternative that works naturally with Astro?
- Can I keep a single Astro deployment and still have a real admin panel?
