There is no single “best Astro CMS” for every project. The right choice depends on how your team edits content, deploys sites, and balances developer control against editor autonomy.

EmDash is a strong fit when you want an Astro-native CMS instead of a generic headless product.

## Use EmDash When You Need

- An Astro-first architecture
- A built-in admin panel for editors
- Type-safe content access in your Astro codebase
- WordPress-style concepts such as taxonomies, menus, widgets, and previews
- A deployment model that stays close to your site instead of splitting CMS and frontend into separate systems

## EmDash Is Probably Not the Best Fit When

- You need an established, massive third-party plugin ecosystem on day one
- You are happy with a fully headless CMS plus API-driven frontend model
- Your project is not based on Astro

## A Practical Astro CMS Checklist

When comparing EmDash with other Astro CMS options, ask:

1. Do editors need a real admin workflow?
2. Does the frontend already live in Astro?
3. Do we want runtime content updates without rebuild-heavy publishing?
4. Do we care about TypeScript ergonomics and integrated content access?
5. Are WordPress-like content management patterns useful for this team?

If most of those answers are yes, EmDash is worth evaluating seriously.
